Goal

The goal of this project is to analyze traveler behavior, spending trends, and trip characteristics to help a travel agency optimize destination offerings, pricing strategies, and marketing focus.

Setup

The project began with data cleaning to prepare the dataset for accurate analysis. This included:

  • Removing blanks to prevent formula errors and improve sorting/filtering.
  • Cleaning the Transportation_Type column by standardizing values (“Subway” → “Train”, “Plane” → “Airplane”).
  • Creating an Age_Group column for demographic analysis.
  • Ensuring correct data types and no missing values.

The dataset originally had 140 records; after cleaning, 137 valid records remained.

Fig 1. Cleaned dataset after preprocessing

Analysis

With the cleaned dataset, exploratory data analysis (EDA) was performed using pivot tables, charts, and Excel formulas to uncover travel trends, spending patterns, and demographic insights.

Business Questions

  • Which destinations are most visited?
  • What is the average trip cost?
  • Which destinations, transport types, or accommodations are most expensive?
  • Do longer trips relate to specific destinations or traveler groups?
  • Which transportation and accommodation types are most preferred?
  • What is the total revenue generated by all trips?
  • Which traveler segments bring in the highest revenue?
  • Do spending habits differ by age, gender, or nationality?

Fig 2. Pivot tables and charts used for analysis

To improve usability, a dashboard was created showcasing key insights, with slicers enabling filtering by nationality, age group, destination, and more.

Fig 3. Travel Patterns & Customer Preferences Dashboard

Key Findings

  • Most Visited Destinations: Highest demand locations.
  • Average Trip Cost: Spending behavior across groups.
  • Most Expensive Segments: Premium travel segments.
  • Trip Duration Trends: Variations across demographics.
  • Demographic Insights: Spending by age, gender, nationality.
  • Transportation Preferences: Most used modes.
  • Accommodation Preferences: Popular lodging types.
  • Total Revenue: Total earnings from all trips.
  • Top-Performing Segments: Highest revenue contributors.

Recommendations

  • Focus on high-traffic and high-revenue destinations.
  • Improve pricing strategy for premium accommodations.
  • Create targeted campaigns for high-spending groups.
  • Offer affordable bundles for cost-sensitive travelers.
  • Partner with transport and accommodation providers.
  • Provide loyalty incentives to long-trip travelers.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights essential travel patterns, spending behaviors, and revenue-generating segments. These insights enable the agency to refine marketing strategies, personalize travel packages, and maximize profitability while improving customer experience.
