Travel Patterns & Customer Insights (Excel Project)

Analyzing travel behavior, spending patterns, and customer preferences using Excel to uncover insights for better decision-making.

Tools: Excel

Goal

The goal of this project is to analyze traveler behavior, spending trends, and trip characteristics to help a travel agency optimize destination offerings, pricing strategies, and marketing focus.

Setup

The project began with data cleaning to prepare the dataset for accurate analysis. This included:

  • Removing blanks to prevent formula errors and improve sorting/filtering.
  • Cleaning the Transportation_Type column by standardizing values (“Subway” → “Train”, “Plane” → “Airplane”).
  • Creating an Age_Group field for better demographic segmentation.
  • Ensuring correct data types and verifying no missing values.

The dataset originally had 140 records; after cleaning, 137 valid records remained.

Fig 1. Cleaned dataset after processing.

Analysis

With the cleaned dataset, exploratory data analysis (EDA) was performed using pivot tables, charts, and Excel formulas to uncover travel trends, spending patterns, and demographic insights.

Business Questions

  • Which destinations are most visited?
  • What is the average trip cost?
  • Which destinations, transport types, or accommodations are most expensive?
  • Do longer trips relate to specific destinations or traveler groups?
  • Which transportation and accommodation types are most preferred?
  • What is the total revenue generated by all trips?
  • Which traveler segments bring in the highest revenue?
  • Do spending habits differ by age, gender, or nationality?

Fig 2. Pivot tables and charts used for analysis.

To improve usability, a dashboard was created showcasing all key insights, with slicers enabling filtering by nationality, age group, destination, and more.

Fig 3. Travel Patterns & Customer Preferences Dashboard.

Key Findings

  • Most Visited Destinations: Highlights demand hotspots.
  • Average Trip Cost: Shows spending behavior across traveler types.
  • Most Expensive Segments: Premium destinations, transport modes, and accommodations.
  • Trip Duration Trends: How trip length varies by demographics and destination.
  • Demographic Insights: Spending patterns by gender, age group, and nationality.
  • Transportation Preferences: Most frequently used modes.
  • Accommodation Preferences: Most popular lodging types.
  • Total Revenue: Full revenue generated by all trips.
  • Top-Performing Segments: Who brings in the most revenue and why.

Recommendations

  • Focus marketing efforts on high-traffic and high-revenue destinations.
  • Review and optimize pricing for premium destinations and accommodations.
  • Create targeted campaigns for high-spending demographic groups.
  • Offer budget-friendly travel bundles to low-spending segments.
  • Partner with popular accommodation and transport providers.
  • Provide loyalty incentives for long-trip travelers.
  • Use insights to design personalized travel recommendations.
  • Revamp strategies for underperforming destinations or groups.

Conclusion

This analysis highlights essential travel patterns, spending behaviors, and revenue-generating segments. By leveraging these insights, the travel agency can refine marketing, personalize travel packages, and maximize profitability while improving customer satisfaction.
